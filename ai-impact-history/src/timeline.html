Milestones

Pre-1950 Ideas in mythology, logic and early computing concepts that foreshadow intelligence in machines.
1950 Alan Turing publishes "Computing Machinery and Intelligence" and proposes the Turing Test.
1956 Dartmouth Workshop — AI established as an academic field.
1960s–70s Early symbolic AI, search algorithms and basic NLP systems (e.g., ELIZA).
1980s Expert systems and knowledge-based approaches become common in industry.
1997 IBM Deep Blue defeats Garry Kasparov in chess.
2010s Deep learning breakthroughs (CNNs, RNNs, GPUs) enable major progress in perception and language.
2020s Large language models and multimodal systems become widely used; AI adoption accelerates across sectors.
